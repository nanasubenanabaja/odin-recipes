Mushroom Chicken


This absolutely Succulent dish consists of chicken cooked in a savory mushroom/tomato sauce. It's fairly easy to make and the ingredients can be found at most grocery stores.

The type of mushroom we'll be using for this recipe is the white/common mushroom.

Mushroomy chicken looks fancy enough for a family dinner!

This recipe was taken and translated from Rosita Cocina's YouTube channel.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup tomato paste
  • 1/2 cup white wine
  • 2 cups common mushroom
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 cup chicken stock
  • 6 tbsp oil
  • 1 previously marinated chicken, separated into pieces

Steps

  1. Marinate your chicken. Marinating it for about three hours should be enough, but for Maximum Flavor, you can leave it marinating overnight.
  2. Pour the oil in a deep skillet and brown the chicken. Closing it with a lid will help brown the chicken faster.
  3. Make sure to brown all sides. Check the chicken periodically.
  4. Heat the butter in a pan, and once it's melted, fry the mushrooms for 3-5 minutes. They will shrink a little, so you'll have to be careful with the timing.
  5. Mix the tomato paste, the wine, the chicken stock and the flour in a bowl, with the help of a whisk. Make sure there are no lumps left.
  6. Pour the mixture over the chicken and add the bay leaf for extra flavor. Let it cook until the sauce boils.
  7. Add the mushrooms and mix everything evenly.
  8. Serve and enjoy!!
